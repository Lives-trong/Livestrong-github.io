About elCID

elCID is software for managing patients with infection.

elCID is designed around the needs of clinicians to help them deliver better care.
Better team communication

Improve communication by giving your whole team a clear view of every patient you care for in real time.

Better clinical data

With interfaces optimised for busy clinicians, elCID helps you capture high quality coded clinical data as part of routine care without getting in the way.

Better service monitoring

elCID enables services to monitor their performance in real time with clinical dashboards, custom reports and extracts.

Integrate with other systems

elCID has extensive integration capabilities, allowing you to pull demographics, lab results, electronic prescribing and more, displaying it with the clinical record of your activity for each patient.

Customisable for each service

Clinical services are all unique - and elCID can be customised to support a huge range of workflows, team structures and environments.

Because the tools clinicians use need to adapt to the way care is delivered.

Powerful reporting

Monitor performance with powerful reporting tools at the touch of a button.

Combine pre-configured activity summaries with customizable reports. All with the ability to export the underlying raw data.

elCID is used every day by teams working in the NHS

High quality prospective data capture ?

elCID captures coded clinical data in real time ready for use in audit and research.

Dr Newman, NHS consultant and academic, treats patients who attend the infectious and tropical diseases service at University College Hospitals London.

He wanted software designed for his service to save time, improve data quality, and support the delivery of better patient care.

He chose elCID to integrate clinical data collection across Clinical Infection, Microbiology and Virology at University College Hospitals London.

elCID provides a single platform to record presenting features, significant events/results, anti-infective therapies and outcomes.

Dr Mal Larder Respiratory consultant

Dr Larder, NHS consultant and academic, treats patients who attend the tuberculosis service at the Royal Free Hospital in London.

He wanted software designed for his service to strengthen clinical governance by replacing several paper based systems with a digital system.

He chose elCID to integrate clinical data collection across the tuberculosis service at the Royal Free Hospital in London.

elCID provides a single platform to record presenting features, significant events/results, anti-infective therapies and outcomes. It supports bespoke data queries and complies fully with NHS Information Governance requirements.
